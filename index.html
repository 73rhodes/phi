De Divina Proportione

Van de Graaf's 'Secret Canon' for single-column broadsheet. The text area is not in proportion to the page, because the back margins which normally form a central gutter dividing verso & recto facing pages are incorporated into the main text area. The left foredge, top and foot margins end up being the same as verso/recto book layout, however.

Van de Graaf's canon works on pages of any proportion, which is well-suited to scrolling media like the web. Although they arrived at the construction independently, Van de Graaf's and Raúl Rosarivo's canons are identical, being based on a division of the page into ninths.

Tschichold's "Golden Canon" adapted to a single-column, variable-height broadsheet layout with margins in 3/4/6 proportion. The text area does not conform to the Golden Section because the height is indeterminate, but Tschichold seemed to be as concerned with the proportions of the page margins as with the proportions of the text area.

The resulting modified Tschichold canon can be used with a Golden Rectangle containing an image, for example, to reproduce the proportions of his original construction. The image below is cropped in the Golden Section with margins in the proportion 3/4/6.
